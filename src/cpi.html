What Is CPI & What Does It Mean

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is a metric for how the prices of everyday goods and services change over time. This is computed by comparing the inflation adjusted prices of goods sold today to historical prices. A high CPI is an indicator of an economic recession, and often indicates that the Federal Reserve may lower rates. You can learn more at BLS.
